Data: Trends & Indicators

Human trafficking in Brazil is caused by regional and demographic factors.

Warning Signs & Demographic Risks

Demographic data shows who is most at risk. Men between 18 and 29 are often targeted for forced labor. Women and girls aged 15–22 are affected by sexual exploitation. Reports from organizations like the UNODC discuss plans in action to strengthen prevention efforts. Here is an infographic that highlights some of the warning signs of human trafficking.

Where Vulnerability Is Highest

Geographics play a significant role. This map highlights the most affected states.

The Tri-Border Region

Brazil's borders, which touch ten neighboring countries, also create many transit points that traffickers exploit. This is particularly evident in the Tri-border region, where Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina meet. The tri-border area is known as a hotspot for activity.

These key facts paint a picture of trafficking in Brazil. The dynamics reveal the severity of the issues but also emphasize the urgency to find successful intervention and prevention strategies.
